Jeju Fire Festival
The Jeju Fire Festival, originated from 1997, is a representative festival of Jeju Island that modernizes the livestock culture of Jeju Island. At the Jeju Fire Festival, wildfires are placed on Oreum (Gisaeng Volcano) to embroider the night sky full of red. The sight of red flames swaying along the large hill is spectacular. In addition to the wildfire, they also provide colorful attractions such as the media facade show and the torch parade. From the media facade show, you can see the entire Oreum lighting up on a large screen. From the torch parade, you can see a volcanic fireworks show, which has an important meaning of the birth of Jeju(volcanic island).
